Treatment Overview


Knee replacement, or knee arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure that aims to relieve pain and improve function in a damaged knee joint. During the surgery, worn-out cartilage and bone are removed from the joint and replaced with metal and plastic components that replicate the movement of a healthy knee. It is typically recommended for patients with severe osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or trauma-related damage when other treatments like medications and physical therapy no longer provide relief.


Recovery involves physical therapy to restore strength and mobility. Most patients can return to everyday activities within 3 to 6 months, though full recovery can take up to a year. Knee replacement can significantly improve quality of life by reducing pain and restoring mobility.


Knee replacement surgery is generally considered a safe and effective treatment for long-term relief of joint pain. Advances in surgical techniques and materials have improved the durability of knee implants, allowing them to last 15-20 years or longer in many cases. Patients who undergo knee replacement often experience a dramatic improvement in their ability to perform daily activities, such as walking and climbing stairs, with significantly reduced discomfort. While the procedure carries some risks, such as infection or blood clots, these are relatively rare and can be minimized with proper post-operative care.

Eligibility for Treatment


Eligibility for knee replacement surgery is generally based on the severity of the patient's knee damage and the extent to which it affects their quality of life. Candidates typically experience chronic pain, stiffness, and limited mobility that interferes with daily activities, despite trying other treatments like medications, injections, or physical therapy. Doctors assess the patient’s overall health, age, and lifestyle before recommending the surgery, ensuring they can benefit from the procedure and recover effectively.


In general, candidates for knee replacement:


Have severe knee pain or stiffness that limits everyday activities, such as walking or climbing stairs.

Have not responded adequately to non-surgical treatments like medication, therapy, or injections.

Are generally in good health and able to undergo surgery and participate in post-operative rehabilitation.

Treatment FAQs

+ Can I see my doctor before I decide and what about my post recovery?

Yes, our hospital offer an initial consultation to meet your doctor before deciding on the treatment. For post-recovery, clinics typically provide follow-up care and rehabilitation plans to support your recovery process.

+ how do i know the clinic i am searching is verified

You can verify a clinic by checking its accreditation with recognized medical boards or certifications and reading reviews or ratings from trusted healthcare platforms. Additionally, ensure the clinic's doctors are licensed and have credible qualifications.

+ I can't travel alone so can some family member or friend of mine accompany me?

Yes, a family member or friend can usually accompany you during your treatment.

+ What are the risks involved in knee replacement surgery

While knee replacement surgery is generally safe, potential risks include infection, blood clots, implant loosening, or damage to surrounding nerves and blood vessels. Your doctor will take steps to minimize these risks and ensure a smooth recovery..
